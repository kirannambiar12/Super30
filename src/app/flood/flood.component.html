FlOOD RELIEF(Photo Feature)

Rush for registration: Pranav Kumar receiving the application forms. The week-long course evoked tremendous response right from day one, with more than 2200 students getting enrolled for it.Due to a large number of students, the course had to be run in three different groups – 7.00 am to 8.30 am, 5.30 pm to 7.00 pm and 7.30 pm top 9.00 pm.


The course starts: Anand Kumar imparting lessons to curious group of students


Packed to capacity: The course drew huge response from the students, who occupied every inch of space in the class


For the right cause: IIT, Mumbai professor KD Joshi engaging special class for the students. He came all the way from Mumbai on Anand’s call.


Preparing for relief: Family members and staff packing relief materials for the flood-affected people.


Movement time: Truck laden with relief material being sent to flood-affected districts. Pranav Kumar left with the relief material, as Anand Kumar was busy teaching the students.


Faces of destitution: Children line up for relief material near the truck near Sonbarsa on way to Saharsa.


Distributing relief: Praveen Kumar and Neeraj Pratap Singh, teachers of super 30 taking relief material to the needy people.


Shaken: A woman with her infant in a shanty in the worst-affected Raghopur block of Supaul district, where relief material was taken to the doorsteps of the needy.


Misery knows no bounds: With husband bed-ridden due to illness, an elderly woman waits for help. She was provided with medicines and relief material.


A ray of hope: An elderly laid accepting relief material from Pranav Kumar at Supaul.


Small moments of joy: Pranav shaking hands with a child. Unaware of the catastrophe that has struck, the innocent child exchanges smile.


All concentration: The students taking the test on the last day of the week-long programme.


Winner all: The top 20 students celebrate with the ‘Mathematical Cup’ with Anand Kumar and Pranav Kumar. Each one of them received an innovative mug in recognition of their performance.


Gifts for all: There were no losers after the programme. On completion of the course, all the participants were presented pens as a mark of appreciation.
